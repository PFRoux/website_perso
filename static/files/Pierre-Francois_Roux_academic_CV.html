Research Profile

I study how metabolic states shape gene regulation, chromatin organization and cell fate decisions in aging, cancer and skin biology. My work combines molecular and cellular biology with functional genomics, multi-omics integration, bioinformatics and systems biology.

Selected Research Contributions

  • Developed an integrative view of AP-1-driven enhancer remodeling during oncogene-induced senescence.
  • Established bulk ATAC-seq as a dual genetic and epigenetic assay for cancer and aging-related molecular profiling.
  • Investigated E4F1-mediated pyruvate metabolism as a link between p53 signaling, acetyl-CoA production and chromatin regulation.
  • Integrated skin microbiome and metabolome profiles to identify microbe-metabolite clusters relevant to skin physiology.
  • Combined QTL mapping, WGS, selection signatures and eQTL analyses to prioritize causal genes for adiposity.
